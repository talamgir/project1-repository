The population of whales in the oceans have been decimated by commercial whaling over the last 300 years.

Several species of whales are considered to be "Critically Endangered" by the International Union For the Protection of Nature and are also protected by international law.

Japan, Norway and Iceland are among the nations that continue to engage in commercial whaling.

Whales are also threatened by other human activities, including the dumping of pollutants and the use of nets to catch fish

We have driven the whales to the brink of extinction. Now, only we can save them.
